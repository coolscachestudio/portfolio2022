Transforming the Local Expert experience for Service Providers to the palm of your hands.

The details

Local Expert is a platform offering service providers a comprehensive, flexible platform to generate leads, manage finances, admin and a way for customers to source trusted tradies. Originating as a desktop application, the experience was translated into a native mobile application for ease of use on the go.

2021 — Ongoing

The problem

1/8

Local Expert, and their parent company, Aspire 42, sought to improve business processes whilst maintaining the flexibility to provide new features and offerings as the business grew. My challenge was to acquire a deep understanding of exisiting processes, and to maintain an adaptive mindset. The key business processes that required improvement were:

  • service management through the mobile application
  • the onboarding process for service providers

existing mobile application

Phase 1

2/8

Phase 1 entailed the refreshment of the exisiting mobile application to adhere to Local Expert's branding, whilst adding new features that allowed service providers more flexibility in a highly automated Microsoft Dynamics 365 environment. These included:

  • the ability for service providers to create their own jobs, quotes, and bookings
  • the ability for service providers to add a new customer
  • the ability to set recurring jobs

In order to inact these changes multiple discussions were had with key stakeholders and the design team to gather an understanding of exisiting process and the systems used. Afterwards, I created a service blueprint to consolidate my knowledge and made assumptions on how these changes could be successfully implemented within the current ecosystem.

service blueprint for the proposed service provider job management

Phase 1 updates

3/8

Throughout the process I indentified additional updates that would further enhance a service providers management of jobs, quotes, and invoices, including:

  • the ability to edit a job if a new date or time is requested
  • implementing Google Maps API on the job detail page to make it easier for service providers to find customers
  • providing prominence to 'take a photo' once a job was complete to ensure invoices were being sent to customers, as this was alerted as a current failure by service providers

phase 1 application updates

App store updates

4/8

As development of phase 1 was winding up I was given the task to create preview screen assets for both the Apple and Google Play Store. Screens were created to be multiple device sizes. Additionally, with my knowledge of the application, I wrote all copy to highlight key features.

mobile application launched in Apple and Google Play Store

Phase 2

5/8

Phase 2 was the implementation of the service provider application process to the mobile application. During the integration of the application process, discussions with key stakeholders uncovered key elements that were missing, including:

  • creating an account
  • adding work experience
  • uploading documents (such as Public Liability Insurance)
  • selecting their Local Expert tier level
  • adding bank details

phase 2 application updates

Phase 3

6/8

Phase 3 involved diving deeper into the structure of service providers. As some service providers would be workers/contractors for another service provider on the app, I needed to design a solution that allowed a service provider to manage their employees. This was shortly referred to as a parent/child dynamic.

In order to create a parent/child relationship, certain permissions needed to be added to particular accounts. With all this in mind the following process was created:

  1. Job comes to parent (booking manager)
  2. Parent (booking manager) selects child (quote estimator) to assign quote inspection
  3. Child (quote estimator) adds a quote — both the parent (booking manager) and the child (quote estimator) own the quote record
  4. Upon customer acceptance, the job can be assigned to a second child (labourer)

phase 3 application updates

Phase 4

7/8

Phase 4 moved back to the desktop website to give the customer quote enquiry a refresh. It also involved adding the ability for customers to select a service provider to replace Local Expert team members from manually selecting one for them. To improve the experience for users, updates included:

  • a side panel to display details the customer has already entered
  • service descriptions
  • Google Maps API for address input
  • service provider selection

Through the implementation of strategic UI/UX design, coupled with dynamic systems — the business now has highly automated systems that has lowered staff overheads while offering industry-leading customer services. Continous strategic direction is being provided during my time at Newpath.
